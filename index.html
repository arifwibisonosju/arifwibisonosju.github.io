About Me

I am a researcher specializing in AI-driven digital simulation and digital twin technologies for complex and autonomous systems. My work focuses on integrating reinforcement learning, multi-agent systems, and physics-based modeling to develop high-fidelity simulation environments for cyber-physical systems.

With a Ph.D. in Intelligent Mechatronics Engineering, I have extensive experience in modeling and simulation of autonomous systems, particularly in dynamic and uncertain environments. My research combines data- driven and physics-informed approaches to enable adaptive, scalable, and intelligent simulation frameworks. My current interests include: + AI-based digital simulation and modeling + Digital twin for cyber-physical systems + Multi-agent reinforcement learning (MADDPG, MAPPO, MODDPG) + Autonomous systems and decision-making under uncertainty + High-fidelity and real-time simulation systems I am open to research collaborations in AI, simulation systems, and digital twin technologies across interdisciplinary domains.
